TECHNICAL APPROACH

objectives

AUTOMATION

Objective 1 of ECA aims to address the current lack of access to EC information through developing, testing, and replicating customized software automation programs to enhance court efficiency. ECA will work closely with MOJ, ECs, and the Ministry of Communications and Information Technology (MCIT) to develop custom software, procure hardware, and train staff. The project will ensure that needed infrastructure, financial, and institutional support are present to maintain and continuously improve applications and business processes for long-term sustainability. The focus of Objective 1 is to improve the efficiency of the ECs through automation of manual functions and re-engineering existing automation components to provide the ECs and the MOJ with a modern Case Management System. The CMS will be designed, developed, and deployed a modular fashion with the aim to cause less disruption to the normal business of the Economic Courts as possible.

TRANSPARENCY

Under Article 68 of Constitution of the Arab Republic of Egypt, Economic Courts must meet transparency requirements. Through Objective 2, ECA plans to work with the ECs by mentoring EC leadership and professional staff in analytic methods, performance indicators and statistical interpretation in the justice sector context, as well as assisting to prepare and publish Annual Reports which include detailed statistics. ECA will also work to strengthen MOJ/EC capacity to oversee publication of EC-related legal issue journals, newsletters, etc. to inform judiciary and legal communities of important information and to create identity within the EC system. Through Objective 2, ECA will work with the ECs by mentoring EC leadership and professional staff in analytic methods and preparing and publishing annual reports, including detailed statistics. ECA will also strengthen MOJ/EC capacity to oversee the publication of EC-related legal issue journals, newsletters, etc., to inform the judiciary and legal communities of important information and create an identity within the EC system.

Human Capacity and Institutional Development

Bring About Organizational Change Within the Economic Courts to Support Greater Efficiency and Transparency. Through the activities of Objective 3, ECA will advance human capacity and institutional development of the ECs, and support professional advancement of EC judges, through development of training curricula focused on: EC applicable laws for senior and lower level judges; performance indicators and statistical interpretation managing case delay; oversight on administrative and logistical issues including CMS and other EC activities; automation-related activities for senior court management that includes analyzing reports and screen-based dashboards generated by the ICT applications and making decisions based on that analyses; and soft skills trainings on topics such as change management, time management, leadership, and communications. ECA will, incorporate principles based upon Project Management Professional (PMP) best practices throughout the implementation period. ECA will also provide technical assistance and training to EC clerical staff focusing on automation training, to efficiently use the EC CMS, training on EC court procedures, and soft skills trainings, including customer service, problem solving, and communications. Objective 3 results will advance human capacity and institutional development of the ECs, and support the professional advancement of EC judges through the development of training curricula focused on EC applicable laws for judges, analyzing reports and screen-based dashboards generated by Information and Communications Technology (ICT) applications; and soft skills training courses on topics such as change management, time management, leadership, and communications.

Comprehensive cutting initiatives

ECA has four key initiatives which cut across the boundaries separating the three ECA substantive objectives. Each cross-cutting initiative will develop a plan framework defining their activities and support that they will provide for each Objective. The initiatives include

MONITORING, EVALUATION, AND LEARNING

the MEL Plan and analysis activities capture the project's core implementation objectives and provide meaningful data to be used by senior court management to measure programmatic success.

COMMUNICATIONS, OUTREACH, AND REPORTING

the Communications and Advocacy Strategy will define methodologies and procedures for effective communication between ECA project members, and between ECA and their partners as well as reaching the ECs target markets.

TRAINING

the Training Needs Assessment and Training Plan indicates the types and importance of various courses to be developed, tentative scheduling, and training modes, including in-person, online, and virtual.

GENDER INCLUSION

the Gender Analysis provides an understanding of gender dynamics across key institutions and counterpart organizations and suggests gender inclusion activities to be implemented across all the project objectives.
